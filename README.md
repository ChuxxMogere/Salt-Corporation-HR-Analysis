# Salt-Corporation-HR-Analysis
Employee satisfaction and retention analysis for Salt Corporation; Excel dashboard, pivot table analysis and PowerPoint presentation

## Project Overview
Salt Corporation surveyed 1,000 employees across 5 departments in 2024 
to measure job satisfaction and identify retention risks. As the data 
analyst, I was tasked with cleaning the survey data, analysing patterns, 
and preparing a visual summary report for HR leadership.

## Key Findings
- Overall attrition rate stands at 39.2%, affecting every department, 
  employment type and salary band equally
- Employees scoring 1-2 on satisfaction leave at over 50%, double the 
  rate of employees scoring 3 and above
- Recency of promotion is the strongest retention lever, employees 
  promoted in August 2024 leave at only 28.4% vs 47.3% for those 
  promoted in January 2023
- B performers leave at the highest rate (43.1%), faster than C, D 
  and F performers, a silent talent drain
- Salary is not the driver, only $100 separates average stayers and 
  leavers confirming this is a career progression problem not a 
  compensation problem

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel: Data Cleaning, Pivot Tables, Dashboard Design
- Formulas: IF, AVERAGEIF, COUNTIF, nested IF for salary and age bands
- PowerPoint: Presentation and data storytelling

## Recommendations
1. Implement promotion milestone reviews at years 2-3 before employees 
   reach the danger zone
2. Urgently engage the early 2023 promotion cohort, highest flight risk 
   at 45–47% attrition
3. Address the B performer recognition gap with a mid-tier development 
   program
4. Build an Analyst career pathway, least satisfied position at 2.70
5. Redirect retention budget from salary increases to career development 
   and recognition programs
6. Implement stay interviews at years 2 and 4 to proactively identify 
   flight risks before they leave
